THE UNCANNY COMES TO REST HERE

Melony McLean was born in a blizzard in 1992. Ever since then, she’s despised the cold. She enjoys being cozy inside: drawing, making music and playing her lifetime collection of videogames. Mel is currently a student at Lethbridge College studying Multimedia Production, eight years after earning her Bachelor’s in English at the UofL. Mel has lived in Alberta most of her life and enjoys the welcome winter chinooks. She has worked in mostly retail jobs, specializing in sales. While enjoying talking to folks, she yearns for the end of the day, when she cozies up with her rabbit Sookie and drinks a soda. Melony decided to expand her education after the 2020 quarantine left her desperate for human interaction and new information, having exhausted streaming services. Multimedia Production seemed like the right fit for Mel, offering a variety of streams to choose from to create a new career. Being an only child, she spent most of her time on the computer. While on the clunky desktop she taught herself HTML, created an anime fan website and frequented social sites like MySpace, Neopets and DeviantArt. She enjoyed watching the internet and technology evolve over her childhood. She feels like the Multimedia program is a great way to both relive those memories and carve a new path. After all, the internet isn’t going anywhere!

Artist's Statement

I like to think of the world in extremes. Therefore, my art is either one way or the other. That is, the feeling I want to provoke may differ within the piece itself. One may be both realistic and disturbing, another may be cute but feels slightly off. Faces are the easiest thing to push into the uncanny valley; if one thing is "off" our brains interpret it as wrong. Right now I am focusing on bringing realism to the comic book style. I like realistic eyes, as that's where the viewer is initially drawn. Then as the eye relaxes, the rest of the image comes into focus, slowly bringing in that opposite extreme. I always use photo references and collages; I believe that's what gives my pieces an extra edge. I like my pieces to create more questions than they answer.
